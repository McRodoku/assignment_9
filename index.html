🍂🍗How to make a Thanksgiving Turkey!🍗🍂


To start, you'll need these ingredients:

  • 1 turkey
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) of unsalted butter, softened
  • Salt and pepper
  • Cooking oil or cooking spray

    • Optional things
  • Seasonings (such as thyme, rosemary, sage, etc)
  • Stuffing
  • Cooking twine (for trussing)

    • You'll also need this equipment:

  • Roasting pan with a rack
  • Meat thermometer
  • Aluminum foil
  • Paper towels
  • Basting brush or turkey baster

    • Instructions:

    Thaw the turkey
  • If your turkey is frozen, ensure it's fully thawed before cooking. This can take several days in the
    refrigerator, so plan accordingly. Follow the turkey's packaging for specific thawing guidelines.


    • Preheat the oven
  • Preheat your oven to 325°F (or 163°C for you Non-Americans that happen to be celebrating
    Thanksgiving for whatever reason).


    • Prepare the turkey
  • Remove any giblets and the neck from the cavities. You can use these for making gravy if you'd like.
  • Rinse the turkey under cold water and pat it dry with paper towels.
  • Season the inside of the turkey with salt, pepper, and optional seasonings.
  • If you decided to use stuffing, put it inside the turkey.


    • Truss the turkey
  • If you took the optional step to truss the turkey, use your cooking twine to tie the legs together
    and secure the wings against the body. Trussing helps the turkey cook evenly and keeps it looking neat.


    • Prepare the butter
  • In a bowl, mix the softened butter with salt, pepper, and any additional seasonings you prefer.


    • Rub the turkey
  • Gently lift the skin of the turkey and rub the seasoned butter mixture between the skin and the meat.
    This helps keep the meat moist and adds flavor.


    • Season the skin
  • Rub the outside of the turkey with additional salt and pepper, and any extra seasonings you like.


    • Place the turkey in the roasting pan
  • Put the turkey on a rack in a roasting pan, breast side up.


    • Roast the turkey
  • Loosely tent the turkey with aluminum foil, place it in the preheated oven, and calculate the cooking time.
    To calculate the cooking time, add 15 minutes per each pound of the turkey.


    • Baste the turkey
  • Every 30 minutes, use a baster or brush to baste the turkey with its own juices and/or additional melted butter.


    • Temp the turkey
  • Use your meat thermometer to check the turkey's internal temperature. The turkey is done when it reaches 165°F (74°C)
    in the thickest part of the thigh. For an accurate reading, be sure not to touch the bone with the meat thermometer.


    • Let the turkey rest
  • Once the turkey is to temperature, pull it out of the oven, loosely cover with foil, and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.
    This allows the juices to redistribute and makes the meat more tender and delicious.


    • Carve it up and serve!
  • Carve the turkey with a carving knife and serve it with your favorite Thanksgiving side dishes.


    • Remember, there isn't any exact way that you have to make your Thanksgiving turkey. This is only a basic how-to guide. There are
    tons of other methods and steps you can take to ensure you have a delicious turkey for your friends and family come Thanksgiving.
